Reaction term

The reaction term describes the change in concentration in a certain point. This variation is only affected by the local chemical reaction. $$ \begin{equation} \tag{3} u_t = f(u) \end{equation} $$ To see the properties of this equation, it can be discretized and solved numerically for different values of \( f(u) \). $$ \begin{align*} u_t =& f(u)\\ \frac{u^n_i-u^{n-1}_i}{\Delta t}=& f(u^n_i)\\ u^n_i-\Delta t f(u^n_i) =& u^{n-1}_i \end{align*} $$ This gives a linear equation \( Au = b \) that easy can be solved. Here the Fisher Kolmogoroff is used $$ \begin{equation} f(u) = ru(1-\frac{u}{M}) \end{equation} $$

Carrying capacity

Carrying capacity describes the maximum concentration for each point.

All simulations goes towards their carrying capacity and stabilizes here. The reproduction rate, \( r \), has been set equal to one for all the simulations in order to isolate the effect of varying the carrying capacity. The time from initial conditions to final conditions is quite similar in all cases.

Reproduction rate

The reproduction rate controls the speed of reproduction

This controls the speed towards carrying capacity. A larger reproduction rate will bring the solution faster towards the steady state, which is controlled by \( M \). The carrying capacity is sat to \( M=0.8 \).
